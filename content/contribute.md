Contributions flow through GitHub issues so that proposals, revisions and disagreements remain visible and discussable in public. New entries are submitted to the editorial board through issues, discussed there, and only then accepted for drafting.

<div class="link-grid">
  <a class="link-card" href="https://github.com/charoy/colladict/issues/new?template=new-entry.yml">
    <strong>Propose a new entry</strong>
    <span>Submit a term to the editorial board, explain why it matters for CSCW and add starting references.</span>
  </a>
  <a class="link-card" href="https://github.com/charoy/colladict/issues/new?template=correction.yml">
    <strong>Raise a correction or debate</strong>
    <span>Flag a problem, omission, contested claim or missing source.</span>
  </a>
  <a class="link-card" href="https://github.com/charoy/colladict/issues/new?template=governance.yml">
    <strong>Discuss governance</strong>
    <span>Question editorial rules, moderation, decision paths or contribution workflow.</span>
  </a>
  <a class="link-card" href="https://github.com/charoy/colladict/issues/new?template=translation.yml">
    <strong>Request a translation</strong>
    <span>Propose a new language version or ask for a translation of an existing page or entry.</span>
  </a>
</div>

If the editorial board accepts a new entry proposal, the proposer receives a limited period of time to complete the entry in one or more languages. If that window expires, the board may reopen the issue, reassign the entry or invite another contributor to take it forward.

The editorial board may also commission entries from external contributors when their expertise is especially relevant. Accepted issues can then be turned into pull requests while keeping the path from proposal to publication traceable.
